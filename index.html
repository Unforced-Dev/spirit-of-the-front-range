Front Range Commons

Infrastructure for belonging

A bioregional solidarity network anchored in trust, stretching from Fort Collins to Colorado Springs. We are the people, projects, and places working together to regenerate our shared home along Colorado's Front Range.

"We care for that to which we belong."

Two Entities, One Movement

Our bioregional work is held by two complementary structures: a self-governing network and a nonprofit foundation that supports it.

Front Range Commons

The Network

A self-governing bioregional solidarity network. We are the living membrane through which people of place coordinate, share resources, and tend to our shared home. Once we reach 100+ members, we become a Decentralized Unincorporated Nonprofit Association (DUNA).

  • Participatory membership and governance
  • Collective decision-making on resource flow
  • Commons principles (Ostrom's 8 principles)
  • Peer-to-peer accountability and attestation

Spirit of the Front Range

The Foundation

A 501(c)(3) nonprofit originally founded in 1992, now revitalized to serve as the institutional backbone for bioregional solidarity. We receive, steward, and flow resources toward regenerative work across the Front Range.

  • Fiscal hosting for the network
  • Grant-making and fund distribution
  • Knowledge commons infrastructure
  • Board eventually elected by bioregional assembly

Working Groups

Three working groups are actively shaping the network's emergence. Each has a defined scope and clear next steps.

Communication

How we extend the call and maintain connection across the bioregion. Invitation language, broadcast channels, and the ongoing pulse of good news.

Focus: Email list, calendar, newsletter rhythm, messaging for Spring Equinox opening

Convergence

How we bring people together. The cadence of gatherings, the wheel of the year, and the design of transformative experiences.

Focus: 2026 event calendar, Beltane launch planning, seasonal pulse design

Culture

How we practice right relationship. Ethics and aesthetics, accessibility, transformative justice, and the ongoing tending of our relational soil.

Focus: Values articulation, onboarding rituals, conflict transformation processes

Our Philosophy

We are grounded in a bioregional worldview that understands our mutual embeddedness in this place as the foundation for shared responsibility.

Belonging as Verb

Belonging is not something you acquire - it's something you practice through caring for the land and each other. We belong by tending.

Place-Based Solidarity

A post-partisan political identity rooted in place. We are responsible to each other across difference because we share this land.

Commons Governance

Following Elinor Ostrom's principles: clear membership, peer accountability, graduated consequences, and the ability to shape the rules together.

Mutual Support

Flowing resources to projects that serve the ecosystem's needs. Not charity, but recognition that some have more than they need while others don't have enough.

Pluralistic Spirituality

Non-sectarian but not neutral. We recognize organizing as a ritual act while creating space for diverse cosmologies and practices.

Remain Unintelligible

Being legible to ourselves while remaining somewhat illegible to systems that would co-opt or control. The network is the membrane.
